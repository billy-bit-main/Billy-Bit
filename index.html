billy-bit · doofus space hunter
Doofus Space Hunter
FINISHED
what it is

A game made in Scratch when I was 10. You drag Doofus — a little dog — around the screen to hunt down monster alien doodles. A monster orb in the middle shoots laser bolts at you the whole time. That's the game. It's not complicated.

I called it Doofus Space Hunter. I stand by that.

how it was built

Built alone in Scratch. No tutorials, no help — just me figuring out how to make a dog chase things and a laser orb try to stop it. It's not impressive by any measure. But I made it work, and at 10 years old that felt like a lot.

what it means

I didn't learn much technically from this one. But looking back, this is where the interest started. Something about sitting down and making a thing — even a really boring thing — and having it actually run. That feeling stuck around even when I quit and came back and quit again.

Doofus Space Hunter is the foundation. It doesn't look like one, but it is.

billy-bit · glasshouse mountains
Glasshouse Mountains
FINISHED
what it is

An interactive 3D scene of the Glasshouse Mountains in Queensland, Australia. Click any mountain and an info panel opens — how hard it is to climb, whether it's open, what dangers to watch out for, whether it's safe.

It's meant to be genuinely useful. Not just a pretty scene — actual information for anyone thinking about heading out there.

how it was built

Built with a friend named Ally. She handled the JavaScript — the 3D scene, the interactions, the info panels. I handled the HTML and CSS. Watching what she could pull off with code was honestly the thing that made me want to get good at this.

I didn't know that a project like this was possible before we built it. Now I know it is. That's worth a lot.

what it means

This project is a big part of why billy-bit.com exists. It gave me hope that one day I could build something as cool as what Ally did with the JavaScript. I'm not there yet — but I enjoyed every part of making this, and that feeling is what keeps me going.
